Gabriel Codina

Who Am I?

Welcome to my portfolio. I am Gabriel Codina and this website goes into detail on who I am, my projects, hobbies, and experience.

My passion for programming started when I was in the sixth grade. It was my dream to be able to develop the video games I played. At the time I wasn't able to learn anything till the summer after seventh grade. I went to don bosco tech summer school and took a programming class where I learned the basics of javascript, where they showed me what codeacademy was. I used this to learn javascript very well.

When I went to highschool I joined the App Academy freshman year. Due to my prior knowlege and passion I was at the top of my class where I learned HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I continued to be at the top of my class through all my years of app academy. Through this class I have learnt so much and have completed more projects than I previously thought I could.

Goals

For a while now, I have always had a goal oriented view on life. When faced with a the weighty question, "What is the purpose of life?" I believe that it is to create our own purpose. Therefore, I have created many goals I want to complete.

One day I had a paradigm shift. I realized that a lot of "talent" isn't actually talent. It is hours of work, perfecting their craft. Therefore, it is better categorized as a skill. There is no cap to the amount of skills you can learn in a lifetime. The best way to learn skills is to learn habits. Most of my goals require me to form habits in order to achieve.

.

Some of my goals include going to the gym, playing guitar(still haven't learnt one song), work ethic, scheduling my day. Past goals that I have accomplished include: completing my black belt in hapkido, NJROTC Drill Team Commander, and Squating my bodyweight

Ever since the sixth grade to become a programmer, and my life has become one big journey to reach this goal. I worked countless hours perfecting my craft. I have always worked harder than my peers, in aspiration to be the best.

Travel

Through all the work and goals to accomplish, sometimes I forget what life is all about... having fun. I love traveling, and have been fortunate enough to have had the oppurtunity to visit many places including Japan, Alaska, Dominican Republic, and Mexico.

Out of all my traveling, Japan has to be my favorite place I visited. The culture shift was the biggest here. People were immensely polite. There was also a massive amount of buildings. I have never seen so many in my life. They had a outdoor mall with a big path down the center and shops on each side. This path must have been at least a mile long.

A close second to japan has to be the Dominican Republic. Being half dominican, it has always been hard to come by people of the same ethnicity. This caused me to be suprised by the amount of people who shared my features. It was amazing. The items were cheap, the island is beautiful, and I had many relatives there.

I have been very blessed to have been on many cruises. The best cruise I have been on was to Alaska. There were so many eventful exscursions. My favorite was a helicopter ride over a glacier, which ended with us landing on it, and drinking from the glaciers run off water... Best water ever. I also very much enjoyed eating the best crab ever, by the shore, and viewing the native totum poles.

Hobbies

I enjoy doing some hobbies more than others, but all my hobbies involve working on skills. Some of these skills don't apply to a job, or anything for that matter. But that is what makes them hobbies. I get better at them because I enjoy them. I have dedicated a page to hobbies if you want to know more.

The hobby I have had the longest is martial arts. I have been practicing hapkido for over 9 years and have completed my black belt. This is one of my biggest accomplishments. This is the longest it has ever taken me to reach any of my goals. It taught me that with enough hard work and self disipline you can accomplish anything.

A hobby which I picked purely for enjoyment is cardistry. Not many people know what this is. One youtube search will lead you down a rabit hole of cardistry videos. My definiton of cardistry is doing beautiful flourishes with playing cards. What led me to this hobby? Well, my strange love for spinning items. Ever since sixth grades I have spinned pens and pencils around my fingers in class when I was bored, and actually took time away from class to learn new moves. When I was in NJROTC I was obsessed with Armed Exhibiton Drill(another hobby), which is basically spinning rifles in a military fasion.

A hobby I did not include on my hobbies page, due to it's genericness, is video games. Ever since I was little I remember picking up my first gameboy advanced and not being able to put it down. I played the same video game over, and over, and over again. Something about being included in a fictional journey away from reality strongly appealed to me. In middle school when I picked up the controller and played my favorite zombie game with my friend, we weren't just playing video games, we were a scientist and a russian soldier fighting off hordes of undead. This sparked my great love for making video games. This caused my love for programming.
